Basic Grammar Rules

Here are the fundamental grammar concepts every English learner should know, explained with simple formulas and clear examples.

Subject-Verb Agreement
Formula: Subject + correct verb form
Example: She runs fast.
They run every day.
Articles (a, an, the)
Formula: Use "a" before consonant sounds, "an" before vowel sounds, and "the" for specific things
Example: I saw a cat.
She ate an apple.
The sun is bright.
Adjectives & Adverbs
Formula: Adjective + noun | Verb + adverb
Example: It’s a beautiful day.
She sings beautifully.
Prepositions
Formula: Preposition + noun/pronoun
Example: The book is on the table.
He walked through the park.
Pronouns
Formula: Use to replace nouns (he, she, it, they, etc.)
Example: John is tired. → He is tired.
Conjunctions
Formula: Clause + conjunction + clause
Example: I wanted to go, but I was sick.
She will join if she has time.
